# The Problem

<div class="nh-columns">
<div class="nh-col">

### We have everything we need

Buckminster Fuller envisioned "making the world work for 100% of humanity... without ecological offense or the disadvantage of anyone."

We have the knowledge, technology, and resources to do it.

</div>
<div class="nh-col">

### But technology isn't serving communities

Innovation is optimized for profit, not resilience. AI and robotics will accelerate this. Without local capacity to steer innovation toward human benefit, we get:

- **Increased inequality**
- **Wealth concentration**
- **Deeper tears in community fabric**

</div>
</div>

<div class="nh-callout">Communities need their own labs ‚Äî not to consume technology, but to direct it.</div>

</div>

# How It Works

### Two paths into the lab

<div class="nh-paths">

<div class="nh-path">
<div class="nh-path-label">Path A ‚Äî Municipal / Public</div>
<div class="nh-path-example">

**Lancaster, PA** funds the development of *Rosalie*, an open-source public data mapping service.

**York, PA** sees it, spins up their own instance. They pay for setup ‚Äî not full R&D.

**Reading, PA** does the same. Each deployment costs a fraction of the original.

</div>
<div class="nh-path-outcome">Public investment multiplied across communities</div>
</div>

<div class="nh-path">
<div class="nh-path-label">Path B ‚Äî Industry / Private</div>
<div class="nh-path-example">

**Company A** needs a cheap biogas digester the market hasn't designed. They seed money to Near Horizon and embed their own engineers alongside the lab's resources.

**Near Horizon** owns the project. The work is open-sourced. Company A gets first-mover advantage; the world gets the design.

</div>
<div class="nh-path-outcome">Private funding creates public infrastructure</div>
</div>

</div>
</div>
